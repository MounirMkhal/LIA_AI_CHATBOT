Meet Jasmina

Trailblazer
in the Intersection of
Health, Technology and
Human Potential

A visionary who blends science, compassion, and purpose—reshaping the future with heart and intellect.

Jasmina’s work stands apart, challenging the status quo by putting human potential at the center of progress. With a Ph.D. in Chemical Engineering from Yale and a certificate in Sustainable Business Strategy from Harvard, she blends deep scientific expertise with strategic foresight. Her career spans academic, nonprofit, and corporate sectors, where she has led and invested in projects that prioritize human wellbeing alongside technological advancement.

Beyond her academic credentials, Jasmina is a driving force in health tech and education, serving on prestigious boards like the Connecticut Venture Group, the Bruce Museum, and the New York Art Academy. Currently, she is an active member of the Yale Cancer Center Director’s Council  and the Rockefeller University Council. She’s also an internationally-ranked saber fencer, a licensed pilot, and co-founder of a private fencing club—demonstrating her discipline, resilience, and commitment to pushing boundaries.

Jasmina’s transformative approach combines science, curiosity, and creativity to shape the future. With her diverse background and forward-thinking mindset, she embodies the dynamic spirit of Generation X women striving for excellence across multiple arenas.

A Conversation with Jasmina

An Intimate Chat on Science, Sports, Investing, and the Art of Transformation

Question

Answer

If your career were a scientific experiment, what hypothesis are you testing?

I’m testing the hypothesis that combining rigorous scientific thinking, strategic investing, and the discipline of competitive sports can unlock unprecedented human potential. 

My professional and personal journey has shaped my belief that an interdisciplinary approach can lead to breakthroughs in longevity and well-being that traditional methods might miss.

What are the key elements in your professional toolkit?

Curiosity, innovation, and impact. I apply the scientific rigor from my science and engineering background to identify promising investments in health technology. But it’s not just about financial returns; I measure success in terms of real-world impact on human health and potential.

How do you approach problem-solving?

My approach is like a fencing match — strategic yet adaptable. I always come prepared with a clear game plan, but I’m ready to pivot based on new data or unexpected challenges. Flexibility, honed through years of competitive fencing, has proven invaluable in navigating the complexities of science, business, and investing.

How do you ‘cross-train’ your mind across diverse roles in science, investing, and athletics?

In every role I take on, whether it’s as a scientist, investor, or athlete, I look for the core principles that overlap across disciplines. The analytical rigor from my scientific work sharpens my decision-making in investing, while the resilience and adaptability I cultivate as an athlete help me think strategically and stay composed under pressure. It’s all interconnected: health, tech, and human potential form a dynamic system. This cross-disciplinary approach is the backbone of my work in longevity intelligence. I’m essentially cross-training my mind, connecting dots across fields to stay nimble and forward-thinking in our fast-changing world.

Question

Answer

If longevity were an investment portfolio, how would you diversify it?

I’d diversify across several key areas: physical health (exercise and nutrition), mental acuity (continuous learning), social connections, and purpose-driven work. Additionally, I’d invest in emerging fields like healthtech, AI, and sustainability. The key is balancing high-risk, high-reward ventures in cutting-edge technologies with stable investments in foundational health practices and personal growth.

What drives your curiosity in exploring the intersection of health, technology, and personal development?

I see an incredible opportunity in our rapidly evolving world to harness technology in ways that enhance human well-being. My curiosity is fueled by the potential to bridge the gap between scientific discovery and personal growth, ensuring that as technology advances, we also evolve and cultivate our inner selves.

How has competitive fencing influenced your professional journey?

Fencing has instilled in me resilience, discipline, and mental agility. Every bout teaches strategy and adaptability — qualities that are invaluable in my professional life. Whether I’m navigating complex investment decisions or driving scientific innovation, fencing has reinforced the idea that growth is a continuous journey, and challenges are just opportunities in disguise.

What’s your vision for the future of health and technology?

I envision a future where health and technology are seamlessly integrated, not just to extend life but to enhance its quality. My goal is to create systems that empower individuals to thrive, using technological advancements to support a holistic sense of well-being. It’s about leveraging technology to help us live better, not just longer.

How do you recharge after a long day?

Rest and proper nutrition are essential for me. Just as in fencing or investing, I know that recovery is key to staying sharp. Taking time to recharge my body and mind ensures I’m ready to tackle whatever comes next.

What’s your go-to stress management tactic?

Exercise is my way of hitting the reset button. It clears my mind, keeps me grounded, and allows me to approach challenges with renewed energy and focus.

What’s the most significant ‘eureka moment’ in your career?

Realizing that science isn’t just confined to the lab—it’s about applying discoveries to make real-world impacts. That moment when theoretical research becomes tangible change in people’s lives has been a driving force behind my work.

What lesson keeps repeating in your life?

Trust your intuition. Whenever I’ve ignored it, I’ve often ended up backtracking. Paying attention to that inner voice has proven to be one of the most dependable guides on my journey.

What would you revolutionize if you could?

People’s attitudes towards well-being. We have the knowledge and tools to live healthier, more fulfilling lives—it’s time to make well-being a priority for everyone.
